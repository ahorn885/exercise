## 0. Scope

This document contains the four operational templates referenced in the DMCA Designated Agent Spec:

1. **DMCA Notice Template** — for complaining parties to submit notices (published at `aidstation.pro/dmca/notice-template`)
2. **Counter-Notice Template** — for users to dispute a takedown (published at `aidstation.pro/dmca/counter-notice-template`)
3. **User Notification Template** — sent to a user when their content is removed under a DMCA notice (internal use; not published)
4. **Intake Acknowledgment Template — optional** — sent to a complaining party on receipt, at AIDSTATION's discretion per DMCA Spec v2 §4.1

Each template is calibrated to the operator-favorable posture established in DMCA Spec v2 — meeting statutory requirements without volunteering additional commitments.

**Posture.** These templates are starting points. The Designated Agent may modify language for specific matters where useful (e.g., complex multi-work claims). The statutory-required elements must be preserved in any modification.

**Status of this document.** Draft for counsel review. Specific phrasings — particularly perjury language and jurisdictional consent — should be verified by counsel before publication.

## 1. DMCA Notice Template

**Use.** Published at `aidstation.pro/dmca/notice-template`. Complaining parties may submit a notice in any form that includes the §512(c)(3) elements; this template is provided as a convenience and is not the only acceptable format.

**Format.** Designed as a fillable form / structured document. May be presented as a web form, downloadable PDF, or plain-text email template.

---

### TEMPLATE BEGINS

**DMCA Notice of Claimed Copyright Infringement**

Submit by email to: `info@aidstation.pro`
Or by mail to: AIDSTATION Pro, LLC, DMCA Designated Agent, 509 Williams Avenue, Cleburne, TX 76033, United States

Under the Digital Millennium Copyright Act, 17 U.S.C. §512(c)(3), the following elements must be included in your notice. Notices missing required elements may not be acted on.

**1. Identification of the copyrighted work**

Describe the copyrighted work(s) you claim has been infringed. If you are reporting multiple works, you may attach a representative list.

[ Field ]

**2. Identification of the infringing material**

Identify the specific material on AIDSTATION's service that you claim is infringing, with sufficient detail for us to locate it. Include URLs, in-product paths, or screenshots with context.

[ Field ]

**3. Your contact information**

Name: [ Field ]
Mailing address: [ Field ]
Telephone: [ Field ]
Email: [ Field ]

**4. Good-faith statement**

By submitting this notice, you state:

> "I have a good-faith belief that the use of the material identified above is not authorized by the copyright owner, its agent, or the law."

**5. Statement of accuracy and authority**

By submitting this notice, you state under penalty of perjury that:

> "The information in this notice is accurate, and I am the copyright owner, or am authorized to act on behalf of the owner, of an exclusive right that is allegedly infringed."

**6. Signature**

Physical or electronic signature: [ Field ]
Date: [ Field ]

**Optional — additional context**

You may include additional information that may help us evaluate your notice (e.g., proof of copyright registration, examples of the original work). AIDSTATION may request additional information at its discretion.

**Important notes**

- Knowingly making a material misrepresentation under §512(c)(3) may expose you to liability for damages under §512(f).
- AIDSTATION evaluates each notice on its merits and acts consistently with §512(c)(1)(C). Submission of this form does not guarantee any specific action or timing.
- AIDSTATION may forward this notice (including your contact information) to the user whose content is the subject of the notice. If you wish your contact information to be redacted in any such forwarding, request redaction explicitly in this notice; AIDSTATION may grant or decline the request at its discretion consistent with applicable case law.
- AIDSTATION is a host of user-generated content and is not a substitute for legal counsel on copyright matters.

## 2. Counter-Notice Template

**Use.** Published at `aidstation.pro/dmca/counter-notice-template`. Provided to a user when their content has been removed under a DMCA notice (linked from the User Notification Template, §3).

**Format.** Designed as a fillable form / structured document.

---

### TEMPLATE BEGINS

**DMCA Counter-Notification**

Submit by email to: `info@aidstation.pro`
Or by mail to: AIDSTATION Pro, LLC, DMCA Designated Agent, 509 Williams Avenue, Cleburne, TX 76033, United States

Under 17 U.S.C. §512(g), you may submit a counter-notice if you believe your content was removed as a result of mistake or misidentification. A valid counter-notice must include all of the elements below.

**1. Identification of the removed material**

Describe the material that was removed and its prior location on AIDSTATION's service.

[ Field ]

**2. Good-faith statement under penalty of perjury**

By submitting this counter-notice, you state under penalty of perjury that:

> "I have a good-faith belief that the material identified above was removed or disabled as a result of mistake or misidentification of the material to be removed or disabled."

**3. Your contact information and consents**

By submitting this counter-notice, you provide:

Name: [ Field ]
Mailing address: [ Field ]
Telephone: [ Field ]
Email: [ Field ]

And you state:

> "I consent to the jurisdiction of the United States District Court for the Northern District of Texas (or, if my address is outside the United States, of any judicial district in which AIDSTATION may be found), and I will accept service of process from the person who provided the original notification under §512(c)(1)(C) or from that person's agent."

**4. Signature**

Physical or electronic signature: [ Field ]
Date: [ Field ]

**Important notes**

- A valid counter-notice will be forwarded to the person who submitted the original infringement notice. The forwarded copy includes your contact information; this is required by §512(g)(2).
- If AIDSTATION does not receive notification of a lawsuit from the original complaining party within not less than 10 nor more than 14 business days after forwarding your counter-notice, AIDSTATION will typically restore the material toward the later end of that window.
- Knowingly making a material misrepresentation under §512(g)(3) may expose you to liability for damages under §512(f).
- The counter-notice process is a statutory mechanism. AIDSTATION's role is procedural; we do not adjudicate copyright disputes.
- Submitting a counter-notice does not affect AIDSTATION's repeat-infringer policy, which considers a broad range of factors at AIDSTATION's discretion.

## 3. User Notification Template

**Use.** Sent to a user when their content has been removed under a DMCA notice, per DMCA Spec v2 §4.4. Delivered via the user's registered email address.

**Format.** Email template. Personalize bracketed fields per notice.

---

### TEMPLATE BEGINS

**Subject:** Important: Content on your AIDSTATION account has been removed under a copyright claim

Hello [USER NAME],

We are writing to inform you that AIDSTATION has removed or disabled access to content associated with your account in response to a notice of claimed copyright infringement submitted under the Digital Millennium Copyright Act (DMCA), 17 U.S.C. §512.

**Content affected**

[BRIEF DESCRIPTION OR IDENTIFIER OF THE REMOVED CONTENT]

**Action taken**

The content has been removed or disabled effective [DATE].

**Copy of the notice**

A copy of the notice we received is attached. The complaining party's contact information has been [INCLUDED / REDACTED PER REQUEST]. The notice provides the basis for our action.

**Your options**

If you believe this content was removed as a result of mistake or misidentification, you may submit a counter-notification under §512(g). Information and a counter-notice template are available at:

`https://aidstation.pro/dmca/counter-notice-template`

A valid counter-notice must include all of the elements specified in §512(g)(3), including consent to U.S. federal court jurisdiction. The counter-notice process has legal consequences. We encourage you to consult an attorney if you have questions about whether to submit one.

**Repeat-infringer policy**

AIDSTATION's repeat-infringer policy applies to your account. We may terminate the accounts of users determined to be repeat infringers in appropriate circumstances. Determinations are at our discretion considering a range of factors, including the number of notices that have resulted in takedown against an account, the nature of the alleged infringements, and the overall conduct of the account. See `https://aidstation.pro/dmca` for the full policy.

**Questions**

Procedural questions about this notice should be directed to `info@aidstation.pro`. Substantive questions about the copyright claim should be directed to the complaining party (contact information in the attached notice, except where redacted) or to your own legal counsel.

AIDSTATION is a host of user-generated content and does not adjudicate copyright disputes. Our role is to act consistently with §512.

Regards,
AIDSTATION Designated Agent

## 6. Gut Check

### 6.1 Risks

- **Template 3 (user notification) repeat-infringer paragraph is the load-bearing public statement.** It must remain non-committal. If a user litigates a termination, this language is the evidence that AIDSTATION did not commit to a specific threshold publicly. Any "softening" edit that adds numbers undoes the operator-favorable posture.
- **Template 2 (counter-notice) jurisdictional consent must be precise.** The "or of any judicial district in which AIDSTATION may be found" alternative for non-US addresses is the statutory formulation; counsel should verify this matches current case law.
- **Template 1 (notice) "AIDSTATION may grant or decline at its discretion" for redaction requests is operator-favorable but must be supportable.** Courts have generally permitted hosts to forward complaining-party contact information to users; the redaction discretion is real but bounded.
- **Template 4 (intake ack) — using it inconsistently may create the impression of differential treatment.** Recommend: either use for all notices or for none. If used selectively, document the reason in the DMCA log.

### 6.2 What might be missing

- **A non-US complainant template variant.** Templates assume US-based complainants. A non-US complainant submitting a US DMCA notice is still valid under §512 but may need slightly different framing.
- **Multi-language versions.** English only. EU launch will likely require at least Templates 1 and 2 in additional languages.
- **A retraction template.** If a complaining party retracts their notice after takedown, the user should be notified that the content is being restored. Not currently templated.
- **A repeat-counter-notice scenario.** If a user submits multiple counter-notices for related takedowns, ad hoc handling.

### 6.3 Best argument against this set

A counter-position: **provide fewer templates, not more.** Some providers do not publish a notice template at all, forcing complainants to construct their own §512-compliant notice. This is operator-favorable in a different way — it filters out unsophisticated complainants.

The defense for providing the templates:

- A published template reduces the volume of substantially non-compliant notices that AIDSTATION must triage
- A published template signals professionalism and reduces friction with legitimate complainants
- The template language is calibrated to AIDSTATION's posture, not to the complainant's; we control the framing

Templates as drafted stand.
