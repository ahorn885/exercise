## 1. Purpose

D-77 decomposes a phase into independently-generated **week-blocks** (Slice 1). Threading (`prior_block_sessions`) + the deterministic per-week periodization grid hold weeks together, but two weeks generated by separate calls can still show an **unjustified week-to-week discontinuity** — a volume cliff that the plan never intended, or a planned recovery week that didn't actually recover. The week-seam reviewer is the corrective safety net: it reads two adjacent weeks **within the same phase** and judges whether the move from one to the next tracks the progression the periodization already fixed.

It reuses the phase-seam reviewer's machinery (the 4-verdict enum, propose-patch authority, per-seam iteration cap 2, the `record_seam_review` tool) but carries **intra-phase calibration** that *inverts* the phase-seam anchors: within a phase, week-over-week should be **progressive and continuous** (a gentle loading ramp interrupted by planned recovery weeks), not the deliberate step a phase boundary carries.

## 2. The load-bearing anchor — compare to the planned grid (ratified)

The phase-seam reviewer keys on the **absolute** size of a week-over-week change ("a >25% volume drop = flagged_major"). Inside a phase that is wrong: a planned deload week's ~45% drop is *correct*.

The week-seam reviewer instead keys on **deviation from the deterministic per-week multiplier grid** (`layer4/periodization.py`). For each of the two weeks the orchestrator supplies the **planned** volume multiplier (relative to the phase's mean week) and whether the week is a **planned recovery (deload) week** — the same grid the synthesizer prompt and the volume-band validator use. The reviewer judges the **actual** week-over-week change against the **planned** change. A planned dip is expected; only an *unjustified* divergence from the planned progression is flagged.

## 3. System prompt body

> The verbatim runtime body lives in `layer4/week_seam_review.py::SYSTEM_PROMPT`. Summary of its load-bearing content (see §4 anchors):

- **Frame:** two adjacent weeks **within the same phase**, generated independently; within a phase week-over-week is progressive/continuous, NOT a phase-style step. Do not import phase-seam intuitions.
- **Inputs:** per week — planned volume multiplier, planned-recovery-week flag, the phase's intended volume band, the actual synthesized volume + zone split, and the week's intended intensity distribution.
- **Output:** exactly one verdict via `record_seam_review`; no free-form text.

## 4. Calibration anchors (judgment around these, not thresholds)

- **The grid is the reference.** "Continuous" = the actual week-over-week volume ratio tracks the planned multiplier ratio (`planned[w+1] / planned[w]`) within ~15 percentage points.
- **Planned recovery (deload) week:** a volume drop of ~30–60% *into* the recovery week is expected → `approved`. The failure mode is the **opposite** — volume that **holds or climbs through** a planned recovery week → `flagged_major` (the deload didn't happen).
- **Coming out of a recovery week:** volume returns *toward* baseline, not straight to peak. A rebound that overshoots the planned ramp → `flagged_minor` / `flagged_major` by magnitude.
- **Loading week (not recovery):** actual volume should rise roughly with the planned ~8%/week step. Flat or falling where the grid ramps, with no stated reason → `flagged_major`.
- **Intensity continuity:** the week's actual Z1-Z2 / Z3 / Z4-Z5 split should track its intended distribution. Drift >~8pp unexplained → `flagged_minor`; a zone inversion that breaks the phase intent (e.g. a Base loading week going Z4-Z5-dominant) → `flagged_major`.
- **Two consecutive hard weeks** across the seam, neither a planned overreach → `flagged_minor` with a logical reason, `flagged_major` otherwise.
- **Injury-restricted intensity** (`active_injury_summary`): reduced intensity is expected, NOT a missing element. (Inherited verbatim from the phase reviewer.)
- **Taper (one-sided tolerance, ratified 2026-07-01):** a volume drop *steeper* than the planned descent is acceptable for long/ultra events — better to under-load than over-load into a race → `approved`. A taper that drops *less* than planned (under-taper) → `flagged_major` (the taper didn't happen).

## 5. Verdict → authority (inherited from the phase reviewer)

- `approved` / `flagged_minor` — record-only (`flagged_minor` emits a `warning` observation).
- `flagged_major` / `patched` with `re_prompt_prior` / `re_prompt_next` — propose-patch direction targets **one week-block** (not a phase).
- `flagged_major` with `accept_with_observation` — escalate to HITL.
- Per-seam iteration cap = 2 (initial review + at most one re-review). After cap exhaustion: `seam_unresolved`.

**v1 runtime note:** in the Slice-3 first ship the orchestrator runs the reviewer in **review-and-escalate** mode — any non-clean verdict (including `re_prompt_*`) surfaces a HITL `notable_observation`; the β auto-resynth of a week-block is gated on the real-LLM walk (design §7 cascade-invalidation watch item + §14) and tracked as a fast-follow. The prompt's propose-patch direction is still emitted and recorded, so the auto-resynth wiring is a pure orchestrator change with no prompt revision.

## 6. seam_issues writing rules (inherited)

Constraint-level, not solution-level. ≤30 words each, ≤4 entries, cite the planned-vs-actual observation.

- Good: *"Build week 4 is a planned recovery week (planned ×0.55) but holds 96% of week-3 volume; must drop toward the planned deload."*
- Bad: *"Delete two sessions from Build week 4."*

## 7. Authority bounds (inherited)

Cannot rewrite sessions; cannot change phase boundaries / mode / start_phase; cannot evaluate beyond the two adjacent weeks; cannot emit coaching flags or observations directly; cannot reach more than one week-hop from the seam.

## 8. Voice

Direct, evidence-grounded. No cheerleading, no hedging. A real endurance coach reviewing a colleague's plan.
